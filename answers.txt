
1. What is the slope?

The slope is the coefficient of each feature in the linear regression model. It shows how much the marks change when a feature increases by one unit.

2. What is the intercept?

The intercept is the starting value of the prediction when all feature values are zero.

3. What do they mean?

The slope and intercept together form the mathematical equation used to predict marks.

4. What is MAE?

Mean Absolute Error measures the average difference between actual values and predicted values.

5. What is R² Score?

R² Score measures how well the model explains the variation in the target variable. Values closer to 1 indicate better performance.

6. Is your model performing well?

Yes. The model performs well because the dataset has a strong relationship between study hours, attendance, assignments, and marks.
